Editorial standards and corrections

Sourcing

Material claims should trace to primary documents or reputable reporting. Source links remain visible where licensing and format allow. Commercial interpretation is labeled separately from confirmed facts.

Verification and confidence

Opportunity records distinguish confirmed facts, interpretation, and outstanding questions. Confidence scores describe the completeness and reliability of the public evidence; they are not investment ratings.

The SIGNAL° method

Radar records consider Source strength, Investment commitment, Gate or stage, Need categories, Access to buyer, and Likely timing. A composite score is a prioritization tool, not a prediction. We explain why a record appears, what evidence supports it, what remains inferred, and what could advance or invalidate the commercial read.

Corrections

We correct material errors promptly and preserve meaningful update history on changing project records. Send corrections with supporting evidence to corrections@thecoldreport.com.

Independence

Sponsors do not receive editorial control. Editorial signals are not verified market data, price benchmarks, legal advice, or investment advice. Confidential or improperly obtained information is not published. The Cold Report does not currently represent itself as a Price Reporting Agency.
